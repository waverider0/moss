1. Cannon

A cannon is fired at an angle of \(45^\circ\) with an initial velocity of \(100 \text{ m/s}\). What is the horizontal distance traveled? (assume no air resistance)

Solution 1

The total time of flight is given by: \( t = \frac{2v \sin \theta}{g} = \frac{2 \cdot 100 \cdot \sin 45^\circ}{9.8} \approx 14.43 \text{ s} \)

The horizontal distance is: \( x = v \cos \theta \cdot t \approx 100 \cdot \cos 45^\circ \cdot 14.43 \approx 1020 \text{ m} \)

Solution 2 (Detailed)

We break the motion into horizontal and vertical components. Since the launch angle is \(45^\circ\), the horizontal and vertical components of velocity are:

\( v_x = v \cos 45^\circ = 100 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \approx 70.71 \text{ m/s} \)

\( v_y = v \sin 45^\circ = 100 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \approx 70.71 \text{ m/s} \)

Time to reach the peak is: \( t = \frac{v_y}{g} = \frac{70.71}{9.8} \approx 7.21 \text{ s} \)

Total time of flight is twice that: \( 2t \approx 14.43 \text{ s} \)

Horizontal distance: \( x = v_x \cdot t = 70.71 \cdot 14.43 \approx 1020 \text{ m} \)
